Co-Director

Christian Giacomel

 

Christian has been involved in the Carleton Engineering Musical since the very first production in 2012. He started off as a mostly nude-crude-dude with no singing or dancing talent…in a musical. But he did have one thing that set him off from the rest of his cast mates. He had…HEART!

 

As sappy and Disney that last sentence was, the most important thing to take out of the C-Eng Musical is that it didn’t matter that you couldn’t sing, dance or even act. It was proving to people that as engineers, we aren’t just calculators, robots and pencil pushers, we also have an expressible and embarrassing artistic side that can only be revealed through silly parody.

 

Through his production, Absolute Zero, Christian hopes to encourage all engineers alike to take the gamble in years to come, those with and without theatrical talent, and to join the C-Eng Musical and let their talent shine.

 

Co-Director

Nick Ogrodnik

 

Nick has been involved in the Carleton Engineering Musical since the first production in 2012. He was that guy in the banana suit – yes, you can say he’s come a long way. He’s always had an interest in writing but theatrics is something new to him. After being cast as Dean Dr. Norton McAffee P. Eng in WD-4D, he discovered his passion for theatre. Nick hopes to use what he’s learned from the directors of future past but also add his own personal flair in the writing and directing of the fourth production of the C-Eng Musical, Absolute Zero.

 

Aside from his life in the C-Eng Musical, Nick is a fourth year student of the Biomedical and Mechanical Engineering program at Carleton. He plans to continue his love for writing and directing after his graduation through continued education at an established Canadian film school with the hopes of making his passion for writing into a career.
